Project Spotlight: PhD Research & Strategy

My doctoral research focused on understanding the fundamental mechanisms of Antivascular Ultrasound (AVUS), a novel, non-invasive cancer therapy. This work required a blend of experimental design, cross-functional collaboration, and deep data analysis to inform future therapeutic strategies.

The Challenge

AVUS shows promise, but its underlying mechanisms were a "black box." Key questions remained: Which blood vessels are affected? What bubble behaviors cause vascular shutdown? How can we monitor this process to ensure safety and efficacy? Answering these was critical for clinical translation.

My Role & Actions

  • Led the end-to-end experimental design of a project modeling tumor vasculature disruption.
  • Collaborated with engineers, physicists, and clinicians to integrate imaging, acoustic, and biological systems.
  • Developed and optimized algorithms in MATLAB and Python for image analysis and cavitation signal processing.
  • Synthesized and presented complex findings at international conferences and in peer-reviewed journals.

Visual Evidence: From Concept to Data

The following images from my research illustrate the journey from designing a novel experimental system to generating and analyzing complex, multi-modal data.

1. Innovative Experimental Design: I designed and implemented this integrated acoustic and microscopy setup to visualize and measure the real-time effects of AVUS in a preclinical model.

2. Generating High-Impact Data: These intravital microscopy images directly show the therapy's effect—vascular disruption—proving the system's ability to generate clear, quantifiable biological data.

3. Quantitative Insight: I translated visual events into hard data, showing that vascular shutdown preferentially occurs in smaller vessels and increases with pressure—a key insight for therapy targeting.

4. Advanced Signal Analysis: Beyond images, I analyzed complex acoustic signals to identify unique cavitation "signatures" that correlate with vascular damage, laying the groundwork for a real-time monitoring system.

The Outcome & Strategic Impact

This research provided a foundational mechanistic understanding of AVUS. By linking specific acoustic signatures to biological effects in different vessel sizes, my work has direct implications for developing safer, more effective treatment protocols and creating the next generation of real-time therapy control platforms.
